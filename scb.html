Context

SCB or Siam Commercial Bank is one of Thailand's largest banking institutions that offers e-banking services through SCB Easy Mobile Application.

Thailand's growing cashless society and increasing access to devices allow e-banking to become more accessible than ever and to accommodate this continuous growth, the current SCB Easy app could use a fresh design improvement.

Problem

Reduce the cognitive load imposing on users by lowering the application's overall UI heaviness, enhancing contrast, and introducing more white space helping to minimize distraction and prevent cognitive overload.

Prioritize visual weight to the application's core banking features, create visually consistent but functionally distinguishable UI elements such as avoiding shape similarities between buttons and ad banners.

Analysis

To improve my understanding, I analyzed the current Easy application and annotate features to maintain or introduce. In addition to suggesting UI improvements that could help speed up user's tasks by lowering the cognitive load of each screen.

Design Solution

Home

The home dashboard is designed to be fully customizable to each user's desired preference.

Users can set their frequently used banking service, add their favorite recipient & payee or view their savings, cards, & mutual funds

And remove any unwanted widget all within their home dashboard.

Account

The account page displays users' saving accounts, balance, cards & credit remaining, collective recent transactions, and new saving goals feature that lets users keep track of their custom personalized goals.

Transfer

Positioning input fields & CTAs at the bottom half of the screen and centered helps retain reachability by optimizing the appropriate thumb zone for both left and right-handed users.

This ensures that users can complete all transfer steps with just their thumb.

In addition to redesigning the recipient bank list to a top-down scroll design which heavily improves scannability by reducing cognitive load and increases friendliness by supporting the display of both their abbreviation and also their more widely-understood full name.

Investment

Relocated as a separate main navigation menu, fund investment is redesigned to visualize users' investments in a chart that allows users to monitor their portfolio progress and view individual funds' growth without the need to rely on 3rd party website/application.

In addition to improving the overall UI contrast, text layout, and bringing visual weight to purchase/redeem/switch core functions.

Investment UX Improvement

As a way to prevent new investors from spending money on purchasing the wrong type of funds that could be unredeemable and mitigate panic when their balance has been deducted but no update is visible, I decided to propose new additional steps and improve the navigations to help inform new investors.

Purchase Alert Modal

Fund Fact Sheet

Credit Card Bill Payment

Credit card bill payment interface is redesigned to be visually coherent with other payment flows.

Displaying user's remaining credit as a donut chart instead of the existing energy bar format could better convey a sense of percentage for users who wish to keep their spending to a specific percent to boost their credit.

Saving Gols

A new Saving Goals feature allows users to create their own personalized saving goals without needing to rely on external budgeting applications or create multiple book banks and transfer repetitively.
